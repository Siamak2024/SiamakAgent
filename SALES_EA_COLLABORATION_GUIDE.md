## 📋 Executive Summary

This guide explains how to use the EA Platform as the **primary collaboration platform** for coordinating sales and enterprise architecture activities around key growth accounts in your DCS (Digital Customer Service) unit across markets.

### Key Benefits

✅ **Unified Account View** - Single source of truth for account strategy, team, activities, and progress  
✅ **Cross-Functional Coordination** - Structured collaboration between Sales Lead, EA, CSM, Solutions Architects  
✅ **Market Alignment** - Best practice sharing and coordination across EMEA, APAC, Americas  
✅ **Customer Success Integration** - Merge EA engagements with Customer Success Plans  
✅ **Activity Tracking** - Log all touchpoints, meetings, calls - never lose context  
✅ **Executive Reporting** - Generate EBR/QBR materials automatically

### The New Operating Model

**OLD WAY (Siloed)**:
- Sales owns accounts in CRM
- EA does architecture work in isolation
- Customer Success tracks goals separately
- No visibility into each other's activities
- Duplication and misalignment

**NEW WAY (Integrated)**:
- **Account Growth Plan** is shared workspace
- Sales Lead + EA + CSM collaborate in platform
- All activities logged in one place
- EA insights directly shape opportunities
- Customer Success goals aligned with EA initiatives

### Your Responsibilities as EA

1. **Account Team Member** - Part of cross-functional team for each key account
2. **Architecture Advisor** - Execute E0-E5 engagements that inform sales strategy
3. **Value Quantifier** - Build business cases that justify opportunities
4. **Knowledge Broker** - Share insights across markets and accounts
5. **Platform Champion** - Ensure team uses platform for collaboration

### Step 5: Execute EA Engagement (E0-E5)

**Who**: EA (you), with Sales Lead involvement  
**Where**: EA Engagement Playbook (linked from Account Growth Plan)

This is your core EA work. The E0-E5 methodology remains the same, but now it's **integrated with sales strategy**:

#### E0: Initiation
- **Sales Input**: Sales Lead provides strategic context, customer priorities
- **Output**: Scope, stakeholders, success criteria agreed with Sales Lead
- **Why It Matters**: Ensures EA work aligns with sales objectives

#### E1: Analysis
- **Sales Input**: Sales Lead highlights competitive threats, urgency drivers
- **Output**: AS-IS assessment, capability gaps, pain points quantified
- **Why It Matters**: Provides ammunition for sales conversations ("You're losing €4M/year due to inefficiency")

#### E2: Design
- **Sales Input**: Sales Lead reviews initiatives for sellability
- **Output**: Target architecture, strategic initiatives (with costs)
- **Why It Matters**: Shapes opportunities - these initiatives become the deal

#### E3: Roadmap
- **Sales Input**: Sales Lead validates timeline against customer budget cycles
- **Output**: Phased roadmap with dependencies and milestones
- **Why It Matters**: Creates multi-year engagement timeline

#### E4: Business Case
- **Sales Input**: Sales Lead reviews value narrative for executive appeal
- **Output**: ROI model, value drivers, business case
- **Why It Matters**: Justifies the investment - makes CFO approval easy

#### E5: Execution
- **Sales Input**: Sales Lead ensures smooth handoff to delivery
- **Output**: Implementation plan, governance structure
- **Why It Matters**: Ensures we deliver what we sold

## 💡 Best Practices for Sales + EA Collaboration

### 1. Weekly Rhythms

**What**: Establish predictable cadences

| Meeting | Frequency | Duration | Purpose |
|---------|-----------|----------|---------|
| Team Sync | Weekly | 30 min | Align on activities, action items |
| Customer Touchpoint | Bi-weekly | 60 min | Progress check with customer stakeholders |
| EA Working Session | Weekly | 2-4 hours | EA progresses E0-E5 work |
| EBR Prep | Quarterly | 60 min | Team prepares for customer EBR |

### 2. Activity Logging Discipline

**Rule**: Log every customer interaction within 24 hours

**Why**: 
- Builds institutional memory
- New team members can get context quickly
- Sentiment tracking reveals relationship health
- Action item tracking ensures follow-through

**Pro Tip**: Use mobile phone to dictate notes immediately after a call while fresh

### 3. Sales + EA Co-Creation

**Don't**: EA works in isolation, then hands off to Sales  
**Do**: Sales + EA collaborate throughout

**Examples**:
- **E0 Kickoff**: Sales Lead joins to provide customer context
- **E1 Analysis**: Sales Lead reviews AS-IS findings before presenting to customer
- **E2 Design**: Sales Lead vets initiatives for customer budget/appetite
- **E4 Business Case**: Sales Lead reviews value narrative for executive appeal

### 4. Customer Language, Not EA Jargon

**Bad** (EA speak):
> "We recommend a domain-driven microservices architecture with event sourcing and CQRS patterns deployed on Kubernetes."

**Good** (Business speak):
> "We'll build a flexible platform that lets you launch new insurance products in 2 weeks instead of 3 months, increasing your competitive advantage."

**Pro Tip**: Sales Lead should review all customer-facing EA deliverables for language

### 5. Link Everything

**In the platform**:
- Link EA Engagements to Accounts
- Link Opportunities to EA Initiatives
- Link Activities to Engagements and Opportunities
- Link Action Items to Team Members

**Why**: Creates traceability and context

**Example**: 
> "Why is this opportunity valued at €2.4M?" → Click linked EA Initiative → See cost breakdown and ROI model

### 6. Celebrate Wins Together

**What to celebrate**:
- EBR went well (customer feedback positive)
- Opportunity moved to next stage
- CSP goal achieved
- Team collaboration score improved
- Action item completion rate high

**How**: 
- Call it out in team sync
- Share in company channels
- Document in platform (milestone achieved)

## 🚨 Troubleshooting Common Challenges

### Challenge 1: Sales Lead doesn't use the platform

**Symptoms**: Sales Lead not logging activities, skipping team syncs

**Root Cause**: Sees it as extra work, already uses CRM

**Solution**:
1. Show value: "This platform helped us close €2.5M deal with SecureLife"
2. Make it easy: "Just 5 minutes after each customer call to log notes"
3. Executive buy-in: Get DCS Unit Head to mandate usage
4. Integration (future): Sync with CRM so no duplicate entry

### Challenge 2: Customer doesn't engage in CSP process

**Symptoms**: Customer cancels EBRs, doesn't provide metrics, disengaged

**Root Cause**: Sees CSP as vendor-driven, not valuable to them

**Solution**:
1. Tie to their goals: "CSP tracks YOUR strategic objectives, not our sales goals"
2. Executive sponsor: Get customer C-level to champion internally
3. Quick wins: Show early value (e.g., "We identified €800K savings in Q1")
4. Simplify: Don't make CSP bureaucratic - keep it lightweight

### Challenge 3: EA work not shaping opportunities

**Symptoms**: Sales creates opportunities without EA input, EA feels sidelined

**Root Cause**: Sales and EA not collaborating early enough

**Solution**:
1. Joint discovery: Sales + EA visit customer together in E0/E1
2. Opportunity review: EA reviews all opportunities >€500K before creation
3. Value case required: No large opportunity without EA-developed business case
4. Shared incentives: Tie EA compensation to revenue influenced

### Challenge 4: Too many meetings, not enough execution time

**Symptoms**: Team constantly in meetings, actual work suffers

**Root Cause**: Over-coordination, poor meeting discipline

**Solution**:
1. Time-boxed agendas: Stick to 30-60 min team syncs
2. Async updates: Use platform for status updates instead of meetings
3. Meeting-free days: Block EA working time (e.g., Wednesdays = no meetings)
4. Decision logs: Document decisions in platform to avoid re-discussion

### Challenge 5: Difficult to scale across many accounts

**Symptoms**: Works well for 1-2 accounts, falls apart with 10+ accounts

**Root Cause**: EA bandwidth, context switching

**Solution**:
1. Tiering: Tier accounts (Platinum/Gold/Silver), adjust effort accordingly
2. Templates: Reuse CSP templates, EBR decks across accounts
3. Specialization: Assign EAs to specific industries or markets
4. Automation: Use platform's report generation instead of manual work
5. Delegation: Junior EAs can execute E1/E2, senior EAs do E3/E4/E5
