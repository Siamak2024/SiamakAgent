      Three-layer view: Capabilities → Systems → AI Agents
      Capability — Layer 1 IT System — Layer 2 AI Agent — Layer 3 System supports Capability Capability depends on Capability
      How to read this graph
      Top row (green): Business Capabilities — what the enterprise does to deliver value.
      Middle row (grey): IT Systems — technology platforms that enable each capability.
      Bottom row (blue icons): AI Agents — automation augmenting specific capabilities.
      Solid arrows point from a capability down to its supporting system or agent. Dashed arrows between capabilities show which capabilities depend on others.
      Tip: click any node to highlight its direct connections. Use the Value Stream dropdown above to focus on a single end-to-end workflow.
      Dependency Impact Simulation
      How to use & interpret
      1) Select a capability/system and click Simulate.
      2) Revenue at Risk: estimated daily revenue/operational value at risk during disruption.
      3) Regulatory Exposure: compliance and penalty risk if process controls fail (LOW, ELEVATED, CRITICAL).
      4) Customer Retention Impact: expected churn/experience impact if service quality drops (MODERATE, SEVERE).
      Use Level 1/Level 2 impact to prioritize resilience investments and mitigation initiatives.

      Step 6
      Application Portfolio Management
      Inventory · Lifecycle · Ownership · Rationalization

      Map the full application landscape — from system registry and lifecycle status to ownership and cost analysis. Identify redundancy, understand technical fit vs business value, and prioritize rationalization actions in a structured portfolio view.

      Instructions

      • 3Visualize the portfolio in the lifecycle kanban and fit matrix
      • 4Set rationalization action: Retain / Invest / Replace / Consolidate / Retire
