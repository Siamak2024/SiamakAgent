# 1. Business Objectives in Enterprise Architecture

## Purpose

Business objectives define **what the organization must achieve** within a strategic cycle. They convert high-level strategy into **clear, actionable target states** that guide both business and IT.

In Enterprise Architecture, they act as the **anchor point** for:

* Capability planning
* Process design
* Application and technology decisions

**Opinion:** If business objectives are weak or vague, everything downstream (capabilities, roadmaps, investments) becomes misaligned. This is where most EA efforts fail.

---

## Key Distinction (Critical for Clarity)

| Concept            | Definition                           | Example                                |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------- |
| **Objective**      | What to achieve                      | Improve digital customer experience    |
| **Capability**     | What the business must be able to do | Customer insights & analytics          |
| **Process/System** | How it is done                       | Feedback workflow / analytics platform |

---

## Example (Cause → Effect Chain)

**Objective:** Increase recurring revenue

Drives:

* **Capabilities:** Subscription billing, Customer analytics
* **Initiatives:** Billing modernization, customer data investment
* **Collaboration:** Business + IT alignment

**Insight:** Objectives create **focus and prioritization logic** across the enterprise.

---

## Why Business Objectives Matter

* Give **purpose** to capability mapping
* Prevent EA from becoming **system-centric instead of strategy-driven**
* Ensure alignment with **real transformation goals**

Without them → capability models become theoretical and disconnected.

# 2. Defining Business Objectives (Practical Method)

A simple, repeatable 4-step approach:

---

## Step 1: Understand Strategic Context

Input:

* Industry
* Company size
* Challenges & opportunities

Identify key themes:

* Growth
* Efficiency
* Customer experience
* Compliance
* Modernization

**Output:** Strategic direction

---

## Step 2: Translate into Outcome Statements

Convert themes into objectives:

Examples:

* Improve digital customer experience
* Increase recurring revenue
* Reduce operational complexity

**Rules:**

* Outcome-focused (not activities)
* No solutions embedded
* Not KPIs

---

## Step 3: Validate with Stakeholders

Ensure:

* Alignment across teams
* No duplication or conflict
* Clarity and relevance

---

## Step 4: Make Them Actionable

Objectives must be:

* Directional
* Measurable enough to guide decisions

Example:

* “Expand e-commerce revenue” (good)
* Not yet a KPI, but actionable
